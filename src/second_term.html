Interval Basics

  • Point vs. interval: A single-value estimate is precise but uncertain; an interval brackets plausible values with a stated confidence level.
  • Known variance: Use Z-based critical values when population variance is available.
  • Unknown variance: Use t-student with sample standard deviation and degrees of freedom.

Planning Samples

Example: For a 95% confidence interval on a mean with σ = 10 and margin of error E = 2, need n ≥ (Z0.025·σ / E)^2 = (1.96·10 / 2)^2 ≈ 97.

Determining sample sizes keeps error within desired bounds and balances cost with precision.
